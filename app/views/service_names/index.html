Why service names are important

Naming your service is the most important decision your service team can have.

It’s important because:

  • it describes what your service does
  • it defines whether users might want to use it
  • it’s how users search for your service

If you name your service well it should be:

  • easier for users to find
  • clearer for users to understand, for example, so you get fewer calls from users asking 'how do I...?'

When to name your service

You should aim to name your service by the end of the discovery phase, when you:

  • have defined the problem you’re trying to solve
  • understand the context of the task your users are trying to do

How to name your service

Good service names:

  • use the words users use
  • are based on analytics and user research
  • describe a task, not a technology
  • don’t need to change when policy or technology changes
  • are verbs, not nouns
  • don't have government department or agency names in them
  • aren’t brand-driven or focused on marketing

Problems with naming your service

If you’re having problems naming your service, it might be because you haven’t scoped your service is correctly.

You might want to expand or reduce the scope of your service if there are many related services, for example if it’s a tax or grant service.

Examples

You can use service names that already exist as examples, including:

  • Register to vote
  • Get help with court fees
  • Renew your passport
  • Find an apprenticeship

For example, before the Register to vote service was created, it was known internally as Individual Electoral Registration System.

Help with court fees was previously called ‘Fee remission’. After doing research with users and court staff, the service team found the name was confusing people. This made the service was harder for users to understand and more expensive to deliver.

Checking the name

User research and tests

You should user research and testing to check that the name of your service allows users to quickly recognise what it does.

You can try tree testing to see if users can navigate from a home page to your service, distinguishing your service from other related services.

Talk to the GOV.UK content team

You should also talk to the GOV.UK content team about the words which users search for that are related to your service.

They may be able to share research which helps you choose the right words to use naming your service.
